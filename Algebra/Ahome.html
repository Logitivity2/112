Equations & Inequalities


An equation is a statement that indicates the equality between two things using symbols.

e.g. If x represents your weight and y your friend's weight and your weight is equal to that of your friend's
then we write x = y. Similarly, if your friend is heavier than you we express this using an inequality: x < y.

In order to solve the problems in this section you will need to make use of the following aritmethic rules:


Commutative Law : a+b = b+a and ab = ba


Addition & Multiplication Properties of Equality : If a=b, then a+c=b+c and ac=bc (for nonzero c).


Distributive Law : a(b+c) = ab + ac
