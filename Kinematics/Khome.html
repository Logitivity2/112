Fundamental Concepts in Kinematics


Kinematics is the study of motion without considering the forces that cause it. The key concepts include:


Distance (d) – The total length of the path traveled by an object, measured as a scalar quantity (having only magnitude).


Time (∆t) – The duration over which motion occurs.


Speed (v) – Distance per time unit ( measured as a scalar quantity).


Reference Frame - The perspective from which motion is observed. For example, a person on a moving train might see
another passenger at rest, while someone outside sees both passenegers moving at the train's speed.


Position (d) – Position is a measurement of a location, within a reference frame.


Displacement (∆x) – The change in position of an object, measured as a vector quantity (having both magnitude and direction).


Velocity (v) – The rate of change of displacement over time (measured as a vector quantity).


Acceleration (a) – The rate of change of velocity over time.
